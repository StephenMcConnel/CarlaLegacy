Pre/Post-Positional Phrases

This section considers the internal structure of PPs. First, we need to see what words can modify PPs. There are normally a few degree words that can modify a whole PP, such as just and right in just around the corner and right to the last second. We can consider words like next and close degree words of this type in next in line and close behind Joe. In addition, certain manner adverbs, such as almost, mostly, squarely, and nearly, can modify PPs, as in almost in the hole, mostly up the tree, squarely on the table, and nearly upon the enemy.
