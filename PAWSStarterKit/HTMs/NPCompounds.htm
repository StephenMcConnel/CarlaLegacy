Nominal Phrase - Noun Modifiers

Many languages are like English, in that they have noun modifiers. Normally, we simply think that all nouns can also be used as adjectives, but a closer look reveals that true adjectives come before the noun modifier, as in large, round syrup bottle but not *round, syrup large bottle. Therefore, we will treat noun modifiers as compound nouns which are written as separate words.

In the English examples above, bottle is clearly the head noun. This is true of all compound nouns and noun modifier constructions in English: the head is the right-most noun in the series.
