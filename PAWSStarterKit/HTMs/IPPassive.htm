Basic, Single-Clause Sentences - Passive Sentences

Most languages also have passive sentences, where the Theme or Patient fills the subject position. The Agent may be optionally expressed in some languages, but not allowed at all in others. If expressed, it may take the form of a PP (as in English with the particular preposition by) or a nominal phrase (DP), depending on the language. In addition, a particular auxiliary may be required in passives (a form of be in English). English examples, including some showing interaction with multiple auxiliaries are:


1. The glass was broken.
2. The glass was broken by the child.
3. The ball might have been hit.
4. The ball might have been hit by the boy.
