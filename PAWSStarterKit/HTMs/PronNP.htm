Pronouns and Proper Names - Pronouns as an NP

Pronouns and also demonstratives, quantifiers, and numbers can stand alone as a nominal phrase in the right context. They cannot normally be modified by possessors, adjectives, or other quantifiers, although they can be modified by a degree word, an article or demonstrative. English examples of this type include the following:


1. all twelve.
2. only he.
3. just two.
4. just those few.

Finally, these categories can also be modified by a partitive phrase (or other PP), as in:


1. only a few of you.
2. just that many of those.
3. only those in there.
