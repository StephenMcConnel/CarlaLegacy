Adverbs and AdvP - Locative Adverbs

Locative adverbs include the English words: here, there, everywhere. In English, location is often also expressed by a prepositional phrase, such as: in the mountains, at San Jose, over there. Many languages allow just a nominal phrase, consisting of a proper noun or description of the place.


Think about how locatives are expressed in your language. For those which are adverbs, are there any degree words which can modify them? In English, this seems to be limited to only (a type of focus construction) and right, as in:

1. only here.
2 only there.
3. right here.
4. right there.

In the lexicon, key the feature locative on all adverbs that express location. You can also add this feature to nouns and proper names which express location.
