Basic, Single-Clause Sentences - Transitive and Ditransitive Sentences

Some examples of English transitive single-clause sentences are:


1. The boy hit the ball.
2. The boy will hit the ball.
3. The boy might have hit the ball.
4. The boy might have been hitting the ball.

If a verb is transitive and it's object must always be present, add the feature transitive to its lexical entry. Do not mark a verb like eat which may or may not have an overt object.

Ditransitives can have the second object expressed as a PP or as a nominal phrase (DP). Some languages, like English, allow both (via Dative Movement as in 4. below). Some English examples of ditransitives are given below.


1. The girl bought the present for her mother.
2. The man sold his car for two thousand dollars.
3. The girl gave the gift to her mother.
4. The girl gave her mother the gift.

Any verbs which require the second object to always be present should be marked with the feature ditransitive. Those which allow the second object, but it is optional, can be marked transitive.
