Simple and Possessed Nominal Phrases

Nominal phrases can be very complex, so they will be divided into four sections in this starter kit. This section concentrates on those with common nouns as the lexical head. We will consider modification by articles, demonstratives, quantifiers and degree words, adjective phrases, and possessors. Section 6 Pronouns and Proper Names will address those nominal phrases headed by pronouns, proper names, demonstratives, and quantifiers. The possibility of modification by pre/post-positional phrases is added in section 7 Pre/Post-positional Phrases and modification by relative clauses is added in section 15 Fully Modified Nominal Phrases.


Because there are so many positions needed for distinct sets of modifiers, we will use the expanded DP (determiner phrase) structure, rather than the simpler NP phrase structure. These more complex tree structures will be built step-by-step from your answers to the questions here. You simply need to be aware that when you look at the tree structures generated, nominal phrases will have DPs on top of the NPs. A simple English example is given here:
           DP 
   ________|________
 Deg               D'
 just     _________|_________
         Dem                NP
        those               |
                            N"
                    ________|________
                   QP               N'
                 ___|____      _____|______
                Adj     Num  AdjP         N'
               first  three    |      ____|____
                              Adj   AdjP      N'
                             fine     |       |
                                     Adj      N

It may be difficult to understand the need for the change in English, because articles, demonstratives and possessors are mutually exclusive, but in many languages all three can occur in a single nominal phrase. (Click here to see more on the DP hypothesis.)

Note that this starter kit adds all the constraints necessary to handle case marking and agreement of articles, demonstratives and adjectives with the noun. If your language does not have such marking, you will simply not need to add the appropriate features to the lexicon, and the constraints will just work vacuously.
