Basic, Single-Clause Sentences - Inflection Features


Sentences normally include some kind of inflection on the verbs and possibly agreement between the subject, object and verb.


Verbs and auxiliaries usually carry inflection features, such as tense, aspect, and/or mood. In most cases, these features are added by affixes. Add the appropriate features from the following chart to the relevant affixes in your lexicon:
