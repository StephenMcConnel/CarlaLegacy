Pronouns and Proper Names - Possessive Pronouns

Many languages also have possessive pronouns which can stand alone, such as yours, mine, theirs, hers in English. These are distinct from those pronouns which can occur in possessor position, such as your, my, their, her, which should have already been handled in section 5.4 Possessors and marked with the case of possessors.

In the lexicon, mark each possessive pronoun with the feature possessive and also

  • all the relevant person (first, first_exclusive, first_inclusive, second, third),
  • number (singular, dual, plural), and
  • gender (masculine, feminine, neuter) features.

In the lexicon, add the feature possessive to the relevant affixes.
