Adjectives and AdjP

In many grammars, numbers, quantifiers, articles, and demonstratives are considered adjectives, but each of them has a different distribution from the qualifying adjectives, so they will each have distinct syntactic categories. This section is limited to considering qualitative adjectives that express size, color, age, texture, form, or quality. One characteristic of this type of adjective is that more than one can modify a noun and in general the order of the adjectives can change (with a slight change in meaning due to the scope of the modification). For example, hard dull brass rods versus dull hard brass rods versus brass hard dull rods, etc. This characteristic of stacking adjectives will be dealt with in the next section by an adjunction rule which allows recursion.


You need to make sure the adjectives in your lexicon will be mapped to the syntax category Adj. Choose one of the following options, depending on your situation, and check it off when it is completed:

  • If you already have adjectives marked with the category Adj in your lexicon nothing more needs to be done.
  • If you have adjectives marked in your lexicon with a distinct category all their own, but it is different from Adj, go to the Category Mapper and map your morphological category to Adj.
  • If adjectives are mixed in with another category in the lexicon, you need to add the feature Adj to each appropriate entry so the category will be changed.
