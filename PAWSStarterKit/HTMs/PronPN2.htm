There is a good chance that you have given proper names a different category than N for your morphological parser, since they may not take affixes at all, or they may take different ones than regular nouns. Check to see what category proper names have in your morphological parser.

In this case, your proper names will have the same defaults as nouns, i.e. third person, singular, and inanimate. Third person should be correct. Any inherently plural proper names need to have the feature plural added in the lexicon (as well as any affixes which mark plural that do not yet have the feature. In addition, any proper names which name people or animals need to have the feature animate added to their lexical entry.

Go to the Category Mapper and map whatever category is used in your morphological parser (possibly more than one mapping will be needed, such as for Biblical names) to N. In the lexicon, mark the affixes with the appropriate feature, either nominative, accusative, ergative, absolutive, or possibly genitive, based on your case system (discussed in section 5  Simple and Possessed Nominal Phrases).
