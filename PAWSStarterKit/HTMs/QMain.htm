Questions

This section considers the main clause and embedded clause varieties of Yes/No questions and content questions. In English, main clause Yes/No questions are formed by moving an auxiliary verb in front of the subject. Other languages use a Yes/No question marker word or particle or special complementizer instead of the fronted auxiliary. This is the case in embedded Yes/No questions in English; no auxiliary fronting is allowed but whether or if fills the complementizer position.

X-bar theory takes the complementizer to be the head of the phrase, so a CP sits on top of the IP for questions. Sample trees for main clause and embedded Yes/No questions in English are shown here.

Some English examples of main clause Yes/No questions are:


1. Are you going to the store?
2. Will Sue buy the present for her mother?
3. Could the boy have hit the ball?
