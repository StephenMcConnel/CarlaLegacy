Basic, Single-Clause Sentences - Intransitive and Copular Sentences

Simple intransitives do not have any complements after the verb. Motion verbs include a location in the form of either a PP or adverb. English examples include:


1. The girl swims.
2. My mother went to the store.
3. My teacher came here.

Partial subcategorization can be done as follows, without entering a feature on every verb: if a particular verb is intransitive and can never have anything but a subject, mark it with the feature intransitive. (Don't consider temporal elements.) If it is an intransitive motion verb that can have a locative PP or AdvP followingpreceding it, add the feature motion to its lexical entry.

Copular sentences can have an adjective, PP, or nominal phrase following the copular verb. Some languages do not require a copular verb or auxiliary to be present. English examples include:


1. The girl is pretty.
2. She seems nice.
3. The girl is in charge.
4. The girl is the leader.
