Basic, Single-Clause Sentences - Auxiliaries

Some languages have auxiliary elements that are separate words, and some can have more than one auxiliary element before the main verb. Examples are given for English transitive single-clause sentences.


1. The boy hit the ball.
2. The boy will hit the ball.
3. The boy might have hit the ball.
4. The boy might have been hitting the ball.

The auxiliary elements need to have the category Aux. If a particular form can also stand alone as the main verb in the sentence, you need to have two separate entries, one with the category Aux and one with the category V. Choose one of the following options, depending on your situation:

  • If you already have auxiliaries marked with the category Aux in your lexicon nothing more needs to be done.
  • If you have auxiliaries marked in your lexicon with a distinct category all their own, but it is different from Aux, go to the Category Mapper and map your morphological category to Aux.
  • If auxiliaries are mixed in with another category in the lexicon, you need to add the feature Aux to each appropriate entry so the category will be changed.
