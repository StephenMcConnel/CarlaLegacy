Pronouns and Proper Names

Let's consider proper names first. In English, some names can stand alone, like Sue or Russia. Others are like compound nouns, such as George Washington. Proper names may also contain articles and/or prepositional phrases, as in the United States of America. Many languages require an article before a name. Appositives, possibly including a possessor, as in my son, David, also occur. In addition, proper names can be modified by adjectives and relative clauses, as in the good, old USA or the Susan Welch that I know.
