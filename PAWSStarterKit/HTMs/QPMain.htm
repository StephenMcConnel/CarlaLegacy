Quantifiers and QP

Most languages have three sets of quantifiers:

  • Those meaning all,
  • other quantifiers such as many or few, and
  • numbers.
We'll deal with each in turn.


Quantifiers meaning all seem to be a category of their own. They usually occur first, in a position distinct from the other quantifiers, but in the same place as degree words like only and just.


Look for examples in your language like the following:

   Example English quantifier phrases:
1 all (of) the children.
2 all five children.
3 all (of) John's young children.
4 only a few sad children.
5 just this many children.
6 just these two bright children.
7 only Sue's three children.

If you have words like all, only, and just which occur either initially or finally in the nominal phrase, mark these words as Deg modifies_NP in the lexicon. Note that this is two separate features with a space between them. The first will change their syntactic category to Deg and the second adds the feature to distinguish them as the type that modifies NPs.

Also, mark the word or words meaning all with the feature plural and the feature quantifier.
