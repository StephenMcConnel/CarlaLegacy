Quantifiers and QP - Cardinal Numbers

Now consider cardinal numbers. They cannot be modified by the degree words like the quantifiers can, though they occur in the same position. Note, however, that cardinal numbers can be modified by ordinal numbers and by next and last, as in the first twenty boys or the next one hundred years. Since these modifiers also act as regular adjectives in modifying nouns directly, they will need to have the syntactic category of adjectives plus a feature to distinguish them.

Further, larger cardinal numbers can be compounds which may be written as separate words, such as one hundred thirty three. In addition, some languages allow two numbers to occur side by side, such as two three to mean a few.

You need to make sure the cardinal number words in your lexicon will be mapped to the syntax category Num. Choose one of the following options, depending on your situation, and check it off when it is completed:

  • If you already have numbers marked with the category Num in your lexicon nothing more needs to be done.
  • If you have numbers marked in your lexicon with a distinct category all their own, but it is different from Num, go to the Category Mapper and map your morphological category to Num.
  • If numbers are mixed in with another category in the lexicon (e.g. adjectives), you need to add the feature Num to each appropriate entry so the category will be changed.
